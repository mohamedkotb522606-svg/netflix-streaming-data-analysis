# 🎬 Netflix Business Analytics Dashboard

> **Turning raw Netflix data into business insights that support smarter content and product decisions.**

---

## 📌 Project Overview
Netflix owns one of the world's largest streaming libraries, but content alone doesn't drive growth. Understanding what users watch, when they watch, and why they stay is what creates a competitive advantage.

This project analyzes Netflix content from a business perspective using **Python** and **Power BI** to uncover patterns in content distribution, audience targeting, ratings, growth trends, and strategic opportunities. 

Instead of focusing only on charts, the project answers real business questions that decision-makers would ask.

---

## 🎯 Business Questions
This project explores and answers the following strategic questions:

### 📑 Content Analysis
* How many Movies and TV Shows are available?
* Which content categories dominate the platform?
* Which countries produce the most Netflix content?

### ⏱ Time Analysis
* How has Netflix's content library grown over time?
* Are Movies or TV Shows increasing faster?
* Which years experienced the highest content growth?

### 👥 Audience Analysis
* Which audience ratings are most common?
* Is Netflix primarily targeting adults or family audiences?

### ⏳ Duration Analysis
* What is the average movie duration?
* How are movie durations distributed?

## 💡 Key Insights
* **Movies Dominance:** Movies represent the majority of Netflix's catalog compared to TV Shows.
* **Geographical Dominance:** The United States contributes the largest portion of available content globally.
* **Content Volume Peak:** Content additions peaked in **2024**, driven heavily by a massive surge in Movie releases, followed by a sharp decline in 2025 and 2026.
* **Data Truncation Notice:** The steep drop in content volume post-2024 is primarily attributed to data incompleteness for the years 2025 and 2026, as the current dataset only captures records up to **June 20, 2026**.
* **Movie-Led Decline:** Looking closely at the trend, the drop is heavily driven by the **Movies** catalog, while **TV Shows** maintained a relatively stable, though slightly lower, release rate during the same period.
* **Target Audience:** Mature audience ratings (TV-MA, R) dominate the platform, indicating a strong focus on adult viewers over family content.
* **Talent Concentration:** A relatively small number of directors and actors appear repeatedly across multiple top-performing titles.

## 📈 Business Recommendations
Based on the analysis, Netflix could consider the following data-driven strategies:
1. **Audit Data Pipeline:** Validate and update the data ingestion pipeline to ensure all content metadata for 2025 and late 2026 is fully populated before making long-term strategic content decisions.
2. **Investigate Movie Strategy Shift:** If the post-2024 decline reflects real-world performance, executive leadership should investigate whether this shift aligns with a strategy of "Quality over Quantity" (focusing on high-budget originals rather than mass licensing).
3. **Balance the Mix Ratio:** Since TV Shows show more stability over time, explore shifting a portion of the production budget from short-lifecycle Movies into high-retention TV Series to maintain stable monthly active user engagement during content dry spells.
4. **Optimize Content Spend:** Invest more heavily in the top high-performing genres and content categories like Adventure and Action.
5. **Global Diversification:** Expand original production partnerships outside dominant countries to capture local markets.
6. **Niche Expansion:** Improve the recommendation engine and content acquisition for underserved family-oriented audiences.
